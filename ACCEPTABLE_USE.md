# Acceptable Use Policy

Tsaagan is a **productivity tool**: it lets your AI agent act like *you* in a web
browser so it can finish routine browser work on tasks **you are authorized to do**.
It is **not** a tool for defeating security, and it must not be used to.

This policy describes how Tsaagan is intended to be used. It does not modify the
[MIT License](LICENSE) (which is permissive), and it is not legal advice — it states
the project's intent and your responsibilities as a user.

## You are responsible for your use

By using Tsaagan you confirm that **you are solely responsible** for what you automate,
and that you will:

- Only operate **accounts and systems you own or are explicitly authorized to access.**
- **Comply with the Terms of Service** of every site you drive, and with all
  applicable laws and regulations in your jurisdiction (including computer-misuse /
  unauthorized-access laws such as the U.S. CFAA and equivalents elsewhere).
- Respect **rate limits, robots directives, and anti-abuse measures** — Tsaagan
  defaults to human-paced interaction and **stops at CAPTCHAs/anti-abuse walls**; do
  not try to work around them.
- Get **human review for consequential actions** (purchases, payments, sends,
  deletions, anything irreversible).
- Handle any data you access **lawfully and ethically**, including personal data.

## Permitted uses (examples)

- Automating **your own** recurring workflows: logging into a dashboard you use,
  pulling a report, filling a form, checking a status.
- Operating **internal tools / SaaS your organization authorizes** you to automate.
- **QA, testing, and accessibility checks** on sites you own or are permitted to test.
- Research and personal productivity within the above boundaries.

## Prohibited uses

Do **not** use Tsaagan to:

- Access any account, system, or data **without authorization**, or to exceed
  authorized access.
- **Circumvent security controls, authentication, paywalls, CAPTCHAs, or anti-bot
  systems** to gain access you would not otherwise have.
- Commit **fraud**, credential stuffing, account takeover, scalping, or impersonation.
- Conduct **denial-of-service**, high-volume scraping that harms a service, or spam.
- Collect or process **personal data unlawfully**, or violate privacy rights.
- Violate any site's **Terms of Service**, or any applicable law.

## Built-in guardrails (and their limits)

Tsaagan is designed to make the right thing the default: it **hands CAPTCHAs and
anti-abuse walls back to a human** (it does not solve them), it **flags consequential/
irreversible actions**, it keeps secrets in your **OS keychain**, and its control
plane is **localhost-only**. These reduce — but cannot eliminate — the potential for
misuse. The responsibility for lawful, authorized use is **yours**.
